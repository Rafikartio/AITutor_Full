    why clustering, not a decision tree?

    A decision tree needs many past labeled examples to learn from — hundreds of prior students marked "weak" or "strong". With just one student's handful of subjects, there's nowhere near enough data for that. Clustering doesn't need labels at all — it just looks at score, hours, and attempts together and groups your subjects by how similar they are. That works fine even with only a few entries.
