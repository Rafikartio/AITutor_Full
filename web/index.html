    Why clustering instead of a Decision Tree?

    Decision Trees are supervised models — they need many pre-labeled training examples to learn from. With only one student's handful of subjects, there isn't nearly enough data to train and test one properly. Mentor instead uses K-means clustering, an unsupervised method that groups your subjects into Weak / Medium / Strong purely from the patterns in score, attempts, and hours — no labeled training data required.
